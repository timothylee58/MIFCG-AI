Anti-Money Laundering and Counter Financing of Terrorism (AML/CFT) — Digital Financial Services Policy Framework
Document No: MIFCG/COMP/2024/0142 Effective: 01 Jan 2024 Review: 31 Dec 2024 Version: 3.2

1. Introduction and Regulatory Basis

This policy framework establishes the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter Financing of Terrorism (AML/CFT) requirements applicable to all digital financial services providers operating under the purview of Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM), pursuant to the Financial Services Act 2013 (FSA 2013) and Anti-Money Laundering, Anti-Terrorism Financing and Proceeds of Unlawful Activities Act 2001 (AMLA).

AI Analysis: Section references AMLA 2001. Note BNM issued updated AMLA guidelines in December 2023 — cross-reference required. Clause 3.4 of the new guidelines may supersede paragraph 1.2.3 of this document.

All reporting institutions, including licensed digital banks, e-money issuers, and payment system operators, must comply with the provisions outlined herein. Non-compliance may attract civil and criminal penalties under Section 24 of AMLA.

2. Customer Due Diligence (CDD) Requirements

Reporting institutions must implement robust CDD procedures as follows:

Customer Tier Transaction Limit CDD Level Verification
Tier 1 (Basic)MYR 1,500/daySimplifiedMyKad / Passport
Tier 2 (Standard)MYR 10,000/dayStandardMyKad + Selfie
Tier 3 (Enhanced)MYR 50,000/dayEnhancedFull EDD

AI Flag: Current Tier 2 limit of MYR 10,000/day may conflict with BNM Circular JPI/BKL/10/4/1 (Nov 2023) which sets the threshold at MYR 8,000/day for digital payment providers. Requires immediate review and update.

3. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) Provisions

Enhanced Due Diligence must be applied in the following high-risk scenarios:

3.1 Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) — Malaysian and foreign PEPs must undergo EDD with board-level approval for account opening and transaction monitoring thresholds lowered to MYR 5,000.

AI Verification: PEP definition aligns with Financial Action Task Force (FATF) Recommendation 12 and BNM's AML/CFT Policy for PEPs (2022). Clause is compliant.

3.2 High-Risk Jurisdictions — Customers or counterparties domiciled in FATF high-risk and other monitored jurisdictions (as listed in Schedule 2 of this Framework) require documentary evidence of the nature and source of funds.

3.3 Correspondent Banking — All correspondent banking relationships require BNM pre-notification and documented senior management approval before establishment.

4. Transaction Monitoring & STR Reporting

Real-time transaction monitoring systems must be maintained with alert thresholds calibrated to the institution's customer risk profile. Suspicious Transaction Reports (STR) must be submitted to the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) of BNM within 3 business days of suspicion arising.

AI Note: FATF Mutual Evaluation of Malaysia (2023) noted deficiencies in STR timeliness. Consider strengthening internal escalation procedures. Recommend adding an internal 24-hour escalation trigger prior to the 3-day BNM deadline.
